# Verified Israeli Cancer NGOs and Support Services

> Disclaimer: Only NGOs verified against their official website, cancer.org.il, or Kolzchut are listed. Do not extend this list with organizations you cannot verify.

## 1. Israeli Cancer Association (האגודה למלחמה בסרטן / ICA)

**URL:** https://www.cancer.org.il/

The main all-ages, all-cancer-types national organization.

### Telemeda information hotline (free, 24/7, trained volunteers)

| Language | Phone |
|----------|-------|
| Hebrew | 1-800-599-995 |
| Arabic | 1-800-36-36-55 |
| Russian | 1-800-34-33-44 |

### Services

- **Transportation** to and from hospitals, with trained volunteers
- **Wig fitting and loans** at Beit Mati (Givatayim HQ) and ~60 branches
- **Cosmetics and makeup volunteers** who visit oncology wards
- **Accommodation** for out-of-town patients near treating hospitals
- **Rights-realization helpdesk** -- phone: **03-5721678**, email: **shikum@cancer.org.il**
- Medical and psycho-social information
- Cancer research funding

Source: ICA hotlines page, ICA contact page, Kolzchut "Information and Support Hotlines for Cancer Patients".

## 2. Larger Than Life (גדולים מהחיים)

**URL:** https://gdolim.org.il/

**Audience:** Children with cancer and their families. A nonprofit founded by parents of children with cancer.

**Name correction:** The correct Hebrew name is **גדולים מהחיים** (Gdolim MeHaChaim). Some internal notes or older references incorrectly write "לרגיש שוב" -- this is **not** the correct name. Always use "גדולים מהחיים".

**Focus:** Long-term support and activities for children diagnosed with cancer and their families (pediatric only, not adult).

## 3. Hayim Association (עמותת חיים)

**URL:** https://www.hayim.org.il/

**Phone:** 03-6120494

**Email:** hayim@hayim.org.il

**Office:** Jabotinsky 138, Ramat Gan

**Audience:** Children with cancer in Israel (pediatric only, all ages under adulthood). Founded 1984. Treats all children regardless of religion, ethnicity, or gender. Volunteer-managed with a volunteer board chair.

**Where they operate:** Inside Israeli pediatric oncology wards across the country's medical centers, as part of the oncology service infrastructure (the org's own framing) rather than an external referral path. (Source: hayim.org.il home page section "ביחד ננצח".)

**Services:**
- **Hospital-department support** -- ongoing material and operational backing to pediatric oncology wards
- **Medical equipment purchase** -- funds equipment for the departments
- **Treatment protocols and clinical staff positions** -- funds תקנים ופרוטוקולים (positions and treatment-protocol research) inside the system
- **Family financial assistance** (סיוע למשפחות)
- **Free shuttle services** to treatments (שירותי הסעות)
- **Welfare workers** (תומכות רווחה) deployed inside hospitals
- **Research support** (תמיכה במחקר)
- **Vacation breaks** (נופשונים) for families of children at high medical risk
- **Recreational programs** -- flying days with the IAF (הטסות בשמי הארץ), Rhodes trips, family events

**When to refer:** Any pediatric cancer case. Hayim complements **Larger Than Life** (גדולים מהחיים) and **Zichron Menachem** (זכרון מנחם) -- the three operate alongside each other rather than competing. Hayim's emphasis is on funding the medical system itself (equipment, positions, protocols) and providing in-hospital welfare and transport; Larger Than Life and Zichron Menachem lean more toward long-term family support, retreats, and emotional care. A pediatric oncology social worker will typically coordinate with all three.

Source: hayim.org.il home page and "אודות" (About) page.

## 4. Zichron Menachem (זכרון מנחם)

**URL:** https://zichron.org/

**Audience:** Children and young adults aged **0 to 25** with cancer, and their families.

**Services:**
- Retreats
- Activities
- Parental support
- Emotional support
- Hair donation program (for wigs)

Source: zichron.org About page; patients-rights.org organization listing.

## 5. Ezer Mizion (עזר מציון)

**URL:** https://ezermizion.org/

**Services relevant to oncology:**

- **Bone marrow donor registry** -- a major Jewish bone marrow donor registry, augmented by routine typing of IDF recruits.
- **Oranit House** -- residential accommodation for cancer patients and their families during treatment. Free of charge.
- Broader services for the sick, disabled, and elderly.
- Large volunteer base.

All services are free of charge.

Source: Ezer Mizion bone marrow registry overview page.

## 6. One in Nine (אחת מתשע)

**URL:** https://www.onein9.org.il/

**Focus:** Breast cancer.

Supports women diagnosed with breast cancer and their "support circle" (family members, friends), including specific resources on workplace rights.

## 7. Patients' Rights Association (האגודה לזכויות החולה)

**URL:** https://www.patients-rights.org/

General patient rights across all conditions. Relevant to oncology primarily for:

- Representation in exceptions-committee disputes
- General patient rights advocacy
- Legal aid referral

## 8. Stop Cancer / Halasartan (חלאסרטן -- עמותה לחולי סרטן צעירים)

**URL:** https://www.stop-cancer.co.il/

**Email:** info@halasartan.org

**Primary contact channels:** WhatsApp icon on every page (bottom-right) + on-site contact form. No public phone line.

**Registered amuta number:** 580603298

**Audience:** Young adult cancer patients in Israel. Distinct from ICA (all ages) and pediatric NGOs (Larger Than Life, Hayim, Zichron Menachem). The branding name חלאסרטן is colloquial -- "חלאס" means "enough" in Arabic-influenced Hebrew slang, so the name reads as "Enough Cancer".

**What they actually do** (peer community, NOT clinical services):

- **לוח מלוות ומלווים (mentors/companions board)** -- structured matching between newly-diagnosed patients and survivors who lived through the same diagnosis. Each mentor has a public profile page with diagnosis, age at diagnosis, and a few lines about their experience.
- **חוכמת המסורטנים (community wisdom)** -- crowdsourced practical guides: "How to prepare for chemo", "What to bring to a treatment session", "Metal taste in mouth", "Hair regrowth", "Cold cap", "I just got my diagnosis".
- **סיפורים אישיים** -- patient personal stories.
- **קבוצות פייסבוק** -- closed Facebook support groups by cancer type and life stage.
- **מדריך למסורטן המתחיל** -- onboarding guide for newly-diagnosed patients.
- **פודקאסט "המחלה"** -- the org's own podcast.
- **יומן מלחמה** -- "war diary" content section spun up after October 2023.
- **חמ"ל** -- the org's wartime situation room.

**Programs and groups (קבוצות וסדנאות):** desert retreat (חלאס במדבר), growth groups (קבוצות צמיחה), AI course (איי.איי.איי), movement/healthy-lifestyle group, climbers group (חלאס מטפסים.ות), creative writing (סדנת כתיבה), painting workshop (עירוי בצבע), pottery (כד דה בולשיט), Baked, "Larger Than Life" survivor group (חלאס נבר-אבר), GameChanger, חלאסטק.

**Arabic-speaking patients:** the WhatsApp group **חלאס סלאמתק** is run by a community lead for Arabic-speaking patients, led by Hanadi Azzam.

**Rights and benefits content** (זכויות / הטבות section): the org publishes its own guides to entitlements and discounts for young patients. This is community-curated, not legally authoritative -- use ICA Rights Center (03-5721678), Patients' Rights Association, or a hospital social worker for any binding rights advice.

**Awareness campaigns** they run: cervical cancer (מעניין לי תיפות), testicular cancer (זמן אשך), colon cancer (#בושה בתחת).

**When to refer:** young adults (roughly post-Zichron-Menachem age, 25+) who need a peer community and survivor mentor rather than (or in addition to) ICA's all-ages services. The peer-matching model is the signature offering and is what differentiates Stop Cancer from other Israeli cancer NGOs.

Source: stop-cancer.co.il home page, contact page, and footer.

## 9. Haverim LeRefuah / חברים לרפואה -- Free Pharmacy Project

**URL**: https://www.haverim.org.il/ (Hebrew) / https://en.haverim.org.il/ (English)

A national medical-aid umbrella that operates two programs particularly relevant for cancer patients:

- **Free Pharmacy Project (פרויקט בית המרקחת)**: collects unused prescription medications via 1,075+ collection points across Israel and redistributes free of charge to patients who cannot afford their medications. Sorted and dispensed by volunteer pharmacists. Source: Jerusalem Post coverage -- https://www.jpost.com/health-and-wellness/article-836161
- **"Save A Life" Funds (קרנות הצלת חיים)**: gathers earmarked donations for individual patient cases the system has failed, including cancer cases. Donors can contribute to a specific named case. Source: https://www.haverim.org.il/funds

Use Haverim when a cancer patient has been refused funding by every other channel and cannot afford the drug out-of-pocket.

## 10. Yad Sarah / יד שרה -- equipment loans for home oncology care

**URL**: https://www.kolzchut.org.il/he/%D7%99%D7%93_%D7%A9%D7%A8%D7%94 (Kolzchut overview, since the org's own site was unreachable from our verification host -- patients should still call directly)

Yad Sarah is a long-established Israeli organization that loans medical-rehabilitative equipment via 100+ branches. Patients typically call about hospital beds, oxygen, wheelchairs, walkers, and other home-care equipment relevant in:
- **End-stage / palliative home care**
- **Post-surgical recovery**
- **Long-term outpatient treatment**

Equipment is loaned for nominal fees. Verify the specific equipment available with the local branch.

## 11. Advance directives and end-of-life NGOs

For cancer patients facing terminal-stage decisions, two Israeli NGOs help with advance directives ("living wills"):

- **LILACH / ליל"ך -- לחיות ולמות בכבוד** (To Live and Die With Dignity) -- https://www.lilach.org.il/living_will/ -- leading Israeli NGO for advance directives and dignified end-of-life
- **Ematai** -- https://www.ematai.org/netivotil/ -- bilingual (Hebrew/English) advance-care-planning resource for Israel, including faith-sensitive options for religious patients

These work alongside the **Dying Patient Law (חוק החולה הנוטה למות), 2005**, which is the legal framework for advance medical directives in Israel. Directives become operative only when (a) the patient is defined as a *terminally ill patient* with under 6 months life expectancy AND (b) the patient lacks capacity. Directives are registered with the Ministry of Health. Source: Kolzchut "Registering a Living Will" -- https://www.kolzchut.org.il/en/Registering_a_Living_Will

## 12. Hospital oncology social worker vs. ICA Rights Center

Two free resources help patients realize entitlements; both are useful, often complementary:

- **Oncology social worker (עו"ס אונקולוגי)**: hospital-employed; psycho-social support, family counseling, practical guidance, hospital-internal paperwork.
- **ICA Rights Center (מרכז מיצוי זכויות של האגודה למלחמה בסרטן)**: an ICA program embedded in 10+ Israeli hospitals. Helps with Bituach Leumi entitlements, basket coverage, exceptions committees, and Form 17. Source: https://www.cancer.org.il/articles/17312/

For complex rights-realization cases (multi-step funding battles, NII appeals), the ICA Rights Center is a strong resource. For psycho-social support and hospital-internal forms, the oncology social worker is the primary contact. Patients can use both.

## 13. NOT a patient-services org (commonly confused)

- **Israel Cancer Research Fund (ICRF)** -- https://icrfonline.org/ -- funds **research**, not patient services. Do not direct cancer patients here for help with treatment or rights. ICRF disburses research grants to Israeli scientists.

## Quick-routing guide

| Patient situation | Primary referral |
|-------------------|------------------|
| Any adult cancer patient, general help | ICA (Telemeda + rights helpdesk) |
| Child diagnosed with cancer, family support | Larger Than Life + Hayim + Zichron Menachem |
| Young adult (15-25) with cancer | Zichron Menachem |
| Young adult (25+) with cancer, peer community | Stop Cancer / Halasartan |
| Needs bone marrow transplant | Ezer Mizion registry |
| Out-of-town treatment, needs accommodation | Ezer Mizion Oranit House OR ICA accommodation program |
| Breast cancer specifically | One in Nine + ICA |
| HMO refused a drug, needs representation | Patients' Rights Association |
| Needs transportation to treatment | ICA transportation program |

## Important notes to agents

1. **Always tell the user to call the ICA Telemeda hotline.** It is free, 24/7, trilingual, and staffed by trained volunteers.

2. **Do NOT invent organizations.** If you are unsure whether an organization exists or serves Israeli cancer patients, do not mention it. The list above is exhaustive for first-line referrals; anything beyond this list must be verified before naming.

3. **Route children to pediatric NGOs, not general NGOs.** Larger Than Life, Hayim, and Zichron Menachem are the three pediatric-specific choices. The ICA also serves pediatric patients, but the pediatric NGOs provide deeper long-term family support.

4. **All these organizations coordinate with hospital oncology social workers.** The social worker will make the right referral based on diagnosis and family situation.
